# Faris — Market Intelligence Analyst Report
**Team:** Competitor Research Unit  
**Date:** 2026-04-16  
**Scope:** MENA edtech competitive landscape, market positioning for Next Academy

## Executive Summary
The MENA EdTech sector is rapidly maturing, characterized by a sharp bifurcation between K-12/test-prep platforms (which capture high volume) and professional upskilling platforms (which capture higher margins, especially in B2B). Global giants like Coursera and LinkedIn Learning are aggressively pursuing MENA market share through AI-driven localization and massive enterprise/government contracts. Meanwhile, regional champions like Almentor and Edraak dominate the asynchronous, Arabic-first video library space. 

However, a distinct vulnerability exists in the market: the gap between pure digital, asynchronous learning (which suffers from low completion rates) and traditional, high-touch offline corporate training (which is unscalable). Next Academy’s strategic opportunity lies in capturing the **hybrid, live cohort-based professional training** segment—delivering localized, culturally nuanced tech and business education that bridges the gap between affordable recorded videos and expensive bespoke consulting academies.

## Competitor Profiles

### Almentor
- **Website:** almentor.net
- **Segment:** B2C and B2B (Enterprise)
- **Geography:** Pan-Arab / MENA focus (Dubai headquartered)
- **Language:** Arabic-first
- **Business Model:** Subscription-based (monthly/annual) and B2B White-label Enterprise packages.
- **Estimated Scale:** Extremely high (multi-million user base, heavily venture-backed).
- **Strengths:** Unmatched library of high-production-quality, exclusively Arabic video content featuring regional celebrities and experts.
- **Weaknesses:** Primarily asynchronous and passive video consumption; lacks deep interactive live cohort elements or rigorous technical bootcamps.
- **Threat to Next Academy:** High (Direct competitor in asynchronous professional skills and soft skills).

### Edraak
- **Website:** edraak.org
- **Segment:** B2C Mass Market and B2B/Government institutional partnerships
- **Geography:** Pan-Arab
- **Language:** Arabic
- **Business Model:** Non-profit MOOC (Free access), subsidized by Queen Rania Foundation and B2B customized platform solutions.
- **Estimated Scale:** Massive user base across the MENA region.
- **Strengths:** Free access removes friction; strong brand trust; institutional partnerships with top universities.
- **Weaknesses:** Course completion rates for free MOOCs are traditionally very low; limited high-touch or personalized feedback mechanisms for complex technical skills.
- **Threat to Next Academy:** Medium (Competes for top-of-funnel learner attention, but not a direct competitor for premium, high-touch paid programs).

### Noon Academy
- **Website:** noonacademy.com
- **Segment:** K-12 and Test Preparation (Qiyas/Tahsili)
- **Geography:** Saudi Arabia, Egypt, and beyond
- **Language:** Localized Arabic
- **Business Model:** Freemium (free basic live sessions + paid Noon Plus premium tutoring).
- **Estimated Scale:** Massive (10M+ users).
- **Strengths:** Incredible success in gamification, peer-to-peer social learning, and highly localized curriculum alignment.
- **Weaknesses:** Strictly focused on K-12; not relevant for professional/adult upskilling.
- **Threat to Next Academy:** Low (Different target demographic entirely, though their social learning mechanics are worth studying).

### Nagwa
- **Website:** nagwa.com
- **Segment:** K-12 schools, teachers, and students
- **Geography:** Egypt (HQ) and UK
- **Language:** Bilingual (Arabic/English)
- **Business Model:** B2B SaaS for schools (LMS/content) and B2C subscriptions for students.
- **Estimated Scale:** High (strong foothold in Egyptian schools).
- **Strengths:** Vast repository of curriculum-aligned worksheets and localized science/math videos.
- **Weaknesses:** Exclusively K-12 focus.
- **Threat to Next Academy:** Low (Differs in core demographic focus).

### Footprint (Egypt)
- **Website:** footprintegypt.com
- **Segment:** B2B Corporate Training / L&D
- **Geography:** Egypt
- **Language:** Arabic/English
- **Business Model:** Customized, offline/hybrid corporate training programs and workshops.
- **Estimated Scale:** Medium (Niche corporate consultancy presence).
- **Strengths:** Deep relationships with Egyptian enterprises (FMCG, Real Estate, Pharma); strong in interpersonal soft skills and leadership training.
- **Weaknesses:** Primarily a traditional consulting/training agency model; lacks scalable, proprietary deep-tech infrastructure or a B2C funnel.
- **Threat to Next Academy:** Medium (Competes directly for Egyptian B2B L&D budgets).

### Global Players (Coursera & LinkedIn Learning)
- **Website:** coursera.org / linkedin.com/learning
- **Segment:** B2C and Enterprise/Government
- **Geography:** Global, with aggressive MENA localization via AI
- **Language:** English-first, but rapidly deploying AI-translated Arabic subtitles and RTL interfaces.
- **Business Model:** Subscriptions, course sales, massive enterprise licenses.
- **Estimated Scale:** Global behemoths.
- **Strengths:** Unbeatable brand prestige (Ivy League certificates), massive breadth of technical and business content.
- **Weaknesses:** Culturally agnostic content; translations can sometimes lack localized industry context; entirely asynchronous.
- **Threat to Next Academy:** High (Heavy penetration in regional enterprise budgets).

### Big 4 Academies (PwC Academy / EY Virtual Academy)
- **Website:** pwcacademy-me.com
- **Segment:** Premium B2B Corporate Training
- **Geography:** MENA-wide, strong Egypt presence
- **Language:** Bilingual
- **Business Model:** Premium B2B training, accredited certifications, graduate programs.
- **Estimated Scale:** High prestige, low volume per cohort.
- **Strengths:** Elite brand reputation; deep integrations with enterprise finance, digital transformation, and leadership.
- **Weaknesses:** Exorbitantly expensive; very low agility compared to nimble edtech startups.
- **Threat to Next Academy:** Low-Medium (Competes for elite enterprise budgets, but prices out the mid-market and B2C segments).

## Competitive Positioning Map

If we map the market on two axes—**Delivery Format (Asynchronous Video vs. Live/Cohort-based)** and **Market Segment (Mass B2C vs. Premium B2B)**:

- **Almentor & Edraak:** Anchor the *Asynchronous / Mass B2C* quadrant.
- **Coursera & LinkedIn Learning:** Span across *Asynchronous* but dominate the *Mass B2C to Global Enterprise B2B* space.
- **Footprint & Big 4 Academies:** Anchor the *Live/Offline / Premium B2B* quadrant.
- **Next Academy's Sweet Spot:** Next Academy should target the **Live/Cohort-Enabled / Mid-Market B2B & Premium B2C** intersection. There is a "missing middle" in the MENA market for highly localized, interactive, live technical and business training that is more scalable than Footprint but far more engaging and high-touch than Almentor.

---

## Underserved Niches

1. **Mid-Market B2B Tech Upskilling in Egypt:** Startups and mid-sized Egyptian enterprises cannot afford Big 4 academies and find Coursera too impersonal. They need localized, live, cohort-based tech/dev training tailored to the Egyptian talent pool.
2. **"Done-With-You" Cohort Bootcamps:** While asynchronous video is commoditized, outcome-driven, live-cohort programs (e.g., intensive 8-week bootcamps with localized mentorship, portfolio building, and job placement) remain incredibly rare and highly prized in the Arabic-speaking market.
3. **Hyper-Localized Soft Skills for the Modern Tech Worker:** Combining tech training with Egyptian culturally contextualized "soft skills" (e.g., how to navigate Gulf clients, remote work communication specifically for MENA devs) is barely addressed by generic Western platforms.

## Strategic Recommendations

| Opportunity | Description | Priority |
|------------|-------------|---------|
| **Pivot Marketing to Live/Cohort Superiority** | Aggressively differentiate against Almentor/Coursera by emphasizing the accountability, networking, and higher completion rates of live, interactive cohort models. | High |
| **Mid-Market B2B L&D Packages** | Develop turnkey, live-virtual corporate training packages targeting Egyptian tech/SaaS companies and scale-ups that need to upskill devs but lack internal L&D mechanisms. | High |
| **Bilingual Hybrid Offerings** | Do not rely purely on translated content. Offer courses where the technical material is English (as required by the industry) but the live instruction/mentorship is conducted in Egyptian Arabic. | Medium |
| **Gamification & Social Learning** | Study Noon Academy's success in student retention. Implement leaderboards, peer-review systems, and study groups for Next Academy's adult professional cohorts. | Medium |
